About Me

Hi! I'm Emran Chowdhury, a Ph.D. student in the Geoscience Department at Virginia Tech. My research focuses on both ex-situ and in-situ CO₂ mineralization, aiming to optimize carbon mineralization processes for secure, long-term carbon storage while assessing their economic feasibility and scalability for real-world applications.

Projects

In situ mineral carbonation in an ultramafic ore body January 2025 - Present

  • Determined the injection scenarios to implement and run at an optimum condition in a pilot project of CO2 storage at Crawford Ultramafic Complex (CUC), the 2nd largest nickel reserve in the world in Ontario, Canada.
  • Developed a reactive transport model in TOUGHREACT (ECO2N) to evaluate CO₂ mineralization potential in ultramafic/dunite formations for the Crawford Ni-Co-PGE deposit.
  • Simulated CO₂ injection and post-injection behavior to analyze plume migration, pH evolution, mineral dissolution, and precipitation.

Carbonation potential of ex-situ flow-through reactor with mine wastes January 2025 - Present

  • Developed a reactive transport model for an industrial-scale flow-through reactor to evaluate ex-situ CO₂ mineralization.
  • Simulated CO₂-water-rock interactions to identify optimal fluid chemistry, mine waste mineralogy, and grain size distribution.

Biogenic hydrogen production from depleted oil reservoir used for geological carbon storage January 2023 - December 2024

  • Conducted HPHT batch reactor experiments using supercritical CO₂, produced water, crude oil, and nutrients.
  • Performed reactive transport modeling and field-scale 3D reservoir simulations to evaluate hydrogen generation potential.

Core sample analysis May 2023 - August 2024

  • Conducted core-flooding experiments on sandstone and carbonate core samples to evaluate petrophysical properties.
  • Operated high-pressure core-holder systems to simulate subsurface reservoir conditions.

SEG EVOLVE Carbon Solution Program September 2025 - February 2026

  • Conducted well correlation, facies interpretation, and petrophysical analysis for the Bunter Sandstone saline aquifer.
  • Developed static and dynamic reservoir models to assess CO₂ storage capacity, injectivity, and long-term containment.

SEG EVOLVE Energy Exploration Program January 2025 - May 2025

  • Performed techno-economic evaluation of the Cadna Owie geothermal prospect in the Cooper Basin, Australia.
  • Estimated project design parameters, conducted sensitivity analyses, and evaluated geological, structural, and economic feasibility.

A Numerical Simulation Study of Single-Phase and Multiphase Reservoir September 2018 - December 2018

  • Developed a numerical reservoir simulation model in Excel using finite-difference methods.
  • Implemented implicit formulation for single-phase flow and IMPES formulation for three-phase black-oil simulation.

Fresh water bearing zone identification using VES January 2017 - May 2017

  • Conducted Vertical Electrical Sounding surveys using the Schlumberger electrode configuration.
  • Processed and interpreted VES resistivity data using IPI2Win for groundwater and lithological interpretation.
